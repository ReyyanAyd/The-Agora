Why we exist

Polarization is not inevitable.

In ancient Athens, the Agora was the heart of democratic life — a place where citizens of all backgrounds gathered to debate, trade ideas, and govern together. We believe that spirit is still possible today.

Our approach

Three pillars of change.

Bridge Dialogue

Structured conversations bringing people across divides into genuine, respectful exchange — guided by trained facilitators.

Counter Misinformation

Research, tools, and media literacy programs that help communities identify false narratives and build critical thinking skills.

Rebuild Civic Trust

Community programs and partnerships that strengthen local institutions and shared norms democracy depends on.

About us

We believe common ground is findable.

The Agora is a nonpartisan civic organization based in Greenville, SC, dedicated to reducing political polarization and misinformation through dialogue, research, and community building.

Our story

Born from a local conversation.

The Agora began in 2023 when a small group of Greenville residents — Democrats, Republicans, and independents alike — grew frustrated with how divided their community felt despite sharing the same streets, schools, and civic life.

They started hosting informal dinner dialogues, then more structured conversations, then workshops. Word spread. The gatherings grew. And what began as a neighborhood experiment became a model others wanted to replicate.

Today, The Agora operates programs in Greenville and partners with organizations across the country, united by a single belief: the divisions that feel so permanent are, in fact, bridgeable.

Our mission

To rebuild the public square.

We work at the intersection of dialogue, research, and education to address the root causes of polarization — not just its symptoms. Our approach is evidence-based, nonpartisan, and grounded in genuine respect for every voice.

We don't ask anyone to abandon their values or pretend differences don't exist. We ask only that people show up, listen, and engage in good faith.

What we stand for
